# How a Costa Rican Village Ran on Lightning

A few years ago a handful of us — locals, expats, surfers, builders —
started orange-pilling our town. Dominical is small: a couple
hundred businesses, dirt roads, jungle on three sides, ocean on the
fourth.

We didn't pitch Bitcoin as an investment. We pitched it as
*payments that work*. Cash is heavy. Card terminals don't work when
the power goes out. Tourists arrive with dollars and locals get
paid in colones — a constant friction tax.

So we built a Lightning wallet, a merchant directory, point-of-sale
software, and a community education program around it. The wallet
is open source. The merchant list is public. The Lightning node is
ours. The whole thing is called **Bitcoin Jungle**.

Today you can pay for groceries, surf lessons, smoothies, gasoline,
yoga classes, and rent — in sats — at hundreds of merchants from
Uvita down to Pavones. Tourists arrive with sats already on their
phone. Locals get paid in dollars-denominated sats and can spend
them the same day at the next merchant in line.

Nothing about this is hypothetical. The sats are real. The merchants
are real. The wallet runs in your pocket.

If you want to see it: `run projects/bitcoin-jungle/demo` for a live
price feed off our backend, or `run projects/lightning-invoice/demo`
for a real invoice you can pay right now.
