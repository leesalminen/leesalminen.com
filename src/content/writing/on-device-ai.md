# Why I'm Betting on On-Device AI

The agent guiding you through this site right now is running on your
machine. No tokens billed, no prompts logged, no internet round-trip
between your question and its answer.

That's not a gimmick — it's the entire point.

Cloud LLMs are a fantastic tool, but they're also a centralised
bottleneck for what is rapidly becoming the most personal layer of
software in our lives. The same arguments I make for Bitcoin — that
the substrate of value should not require permission from a single
intermediary — apply to the substrate of cognition.

## What changes when it's local

- **Privacy by default.** Your queries never leave your device.
- **Offline-first.** The web is patchy in a lot of the world.
- **Zero marginal cost.** The model is already downloaded; inference
  is just compute you own.
- **Composable.** I can ship a tiny agent embedded in any web page,
  no API key, no backend.

## What we're still missing

Local models are small. Context windows are short. Tool use is
fiddly. There's no shared "memory" across pages — each tab is a
fresh brain.

I think these problems get solved in 2026. By the time they do,
we'll look back at "shipping an AI feature" as a synonym for
"shipping a Stripe subscription to OpenAI" the way 1999 felt about
running your own SMTP server.

Build for the world where the model is local. That's the world
we're moving into.
